Steppenwolf started in a church basement in Highland Park with no money, no experience, and no institutional support. The Wooster Group built a world from found objects in a SoHo garage. Plan-B Theatre has operated for thirty years on a micro-budget, producing some of the most respected new work in the country.

None of them were rescued by a foundation. None of them went viral. They persisted.

Every surviving scrappy theatre shares common DNA: a core group of irrationally committed people, a specific artistic identity that is genuinely distinctive, fiscal discipline bordering on asceticism, and the patience to build an audience one show at a time, one audience member at a time, through the quality of the work itself.
